About

Consonants / Glyphs

Given the absence of an official Pyr's cypher (when this was first made at least), one may have decided to make an alternative. This is that alternative. Whilst it functions similarly to a substitution cipher, there are a few notable differences:

  • This cipher uses an impure abjad, not an alphabet (meaning that vowels are represented by optional diacritics, not their own glyphs)
  • There are additional glyphs for certain letter combinations (these being "ch", "ll", "ss", "sh", "tt", "th" and the final "s" of a word)
  • As intended this cypher uses scriptura continua. Meaning there is no space between words.
  • The letter combination "qu" shares a single glyph
The above are meant to implement an aura of confusion as they do not follow the standard writing conventions of common. The glyphs are as follows:

b c ch d f g h j k l ll m n p qu r s s (fin.) sh
ss t tt th v w x y z

Vowel Diacritics

Placement

Diacritics placed above a consonant (or another diacritic) show that that vowel goes after what they are placed on. Diacritics placed below a consonant (or another diacritic) show that that vowel goes before what they are placed on.
